Aykut Erdem · Koç University

The broad goal of my research is to explore better ways to understand, interpret and manipulate visual data*. My research interests span a diverse set of topics, ranging from image editing to visual saliency estimation, and to multimodal learning for integrated vision and language.

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Engineering at Koç University. Previously, I was one of the directors of the Computer Vision Lab at Hacettepe University. Prior to that, I was a post-doctoral researcher in the Computer Science Department of Ca’Foscari University of Venice in Italy, working with Marcello Pelillo. I received my PhD degree in Computer Engineering from METU in 2008, and before that, I obtained my BSc and MSc degrees from the same institution. During my PhD studies, I spent a summer at Virginia Tech in 2004 and I was a visiting scholar at MIT during fall 2007.
